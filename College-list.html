A balanced college list is the foundation of a successful application cycle. Learn the difference between Safety, Target, and Reach schools to manage expectations and maximize your options.

The Three Categories of Schools

1. Safety Schools (High Probability)

  • **Definition:** Schools where your GPA and test scores significantly exceed the typical admitted student profile.
  • **Goal:** A guaranteed acceptance with strong financial aid potential.
  • **Target Count:** 2-3 schools.

2. Target Schools (Good Fit)

  • **Definition:** Schools where your academic profile is directly in line with (or slightly above/below) the median admitted student.
  • **Goal:** These are your most likely acceptances; you feel enthusiastic about attending them.
  • **Target Count:** 4-6 schools.

3. Reach Schools (Aspirational)

  • **Definition:** Schools where your academic profile is below the median, or schools with highly selective acceptance rates (below 15-20%).
  • **Goal:** A potential dream acceptance, recognizing that chances are lower for everyone.
  • **Target Count:** 2-4 schools.

Financial and Emotional Considerations

The "Financial Safety" Check

Ensure that at least **one or two** of your safety schools are also financially affordable *without* needing merit aid. A college is not a true safety if you can't afford the tuition.

The Final List Test

Ask yourself: **"Would I be genuinely happy if my only acceptance was from this school?"** If the answer is no for any school on your list (even a safety), remove it and find a replacement that excites you.
