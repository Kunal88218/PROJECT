The personal essay is your single best opportunity to humanize your application. Follow these essential steps and strategies to tell a story that admissions officers can't ignore.

Phase 1: Finding Your Unique Story

1. Move Beyond Achievements

Admissions officers already see your grades and awards. Use the essay to reveal your **character, values, and growth**. Focus on a small, specific moment that had a big emotional impact on you, not a broad life history.

2. Show, Don't Tell

Instead of writing "I am a determined person," describe a moment where you *demonstrated* determination. Use **sensory details**‚Äîwhat did you see, hear, or feel? This makes your experience come alive for the reader.

Phase 2: Polishing the Narrative

3. Embrace the 'I'

Remember this essay is about *you*. While others may be in your story, ensure the focus always returns to your **reflections, insights, and lessons learned**. This is what colleges are truly looking for.

4. Edit Ruthlessly

After finishing your draft, cut unnecessary words and phrases. Check for passive voice and clich√©s. **Read your essay aloud** to catch awkward phrasing and pacing issues before final submission.
