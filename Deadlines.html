Stay organized and on track! Use this timeline and checklist to ensure you don't miss a single critical step in the college application process.

Your 4-Step Application Timeline

  1. Step 1: Early Research (Junior Year Spring/Summer)

    Finalize your list of **target, safety, and reach schools**. Visit campuses virtually or in person. Create a Common Application account and start drafting your Personal Essay.

  2. Step 2: Documentation (Senior Year Early Fall)

    Finalize test scores (SAT/ACT) and send them to schools. Ask **teachers and counselors** for letters of recommendation. Begin filling out the FAFSA/CSS Profile for financial aid.

  3. Step 3: Submission Phase (October - November)

    Submit **Early Action (EA) or Early Decision (ED)** applications first. Proofread all supplemental essays multiple times. Confirm that your recommenders have submitted their letters.

  4. Step 4: Regular Decision & Follow-Up (December - January)

    Submit all **Regular Decision** applications before the deadlines. Follow up with schools to ensure all components (transcripts, scores, letters) were received.

Common Application Deadline Types

Type Typical Deadline Key Feature
Early Decision (ED) November 1st - 15th **Binding.** You must enroll if accepted.
Early Action (EA) November 1st - December 1st **Non-binding.** You get an early decision.
Regular Decision (RD) January 1st - 15th Standard deadline. Receive decisions in spring.
Rolling Admission Varies (Starts Aug/Sept) Apply early, decisions are made as applications arrive.
