Standardized tests can be intimidating, but with a focused strategy, you can maximize your scores and approach test day with confidence. Here are our top prep tips.

Phase 1: Decision and Planning

1. Choose the Right Test: SAT vs. ACT

Take a **full-length practice test** of both the SAT and ACT early on. Analyze which test format, pacing, and subject distribution (especially the ACT's science section) best suits your natural strengths.

2. Create a Study Schedule (6-8 Weeks)

Don't cram. Dedicate **45â€“60 minutes, 3â€“4 times per week**, focusing on your weakest sections first. Reserve the final two weeks for full-length, timed practice tests to build endurance.

Phase 2: Tactics and Execution

3. Learn the Pacing Strategy

Time management is crucial. Know exactly how much time you have per question for each section. **Never leave an answer blank** (since there is no penalty for guessing on the SAT or ACT), but don't linger too long on tough questions.

4. Review Your Mistakes Religiously

The biggest prep mistake is just taking tests without learning from them. Keep an **"Error Log"** where you analyze why you got a question wrong, then practice similar problems until you master the concept.
